Day 09: The ISRO dials the stars 9 p.m. (IST), April 3rd, 2020

On last night's show Prof. Martin John Rees, British cosmologist and astrophysicist encouraged young scientists to study and create new observations, new theories and new techniques. He shared ideas around his work on cosmic radiation and extreme phenomena in the cosmos, how this century is special as humans have more control than before yet no astronomer believes that we are at the end point. Future evolution is beyond humans.

We discussed science fiction writing and how sci-fi is stimulating for its ideas -first rate sci fi better than 2nd rate science. In the light of this current pandemic he emphasised that we need to prepare better to preserve freedom, privacy and security for our future, and understand that the unfamiliar is not the same as the improbable. As this situation has accentuated social disparity, he hopes it will lead to a political change towards creating a new social order in which we are sensitive to our environment. And finally, how both science and art are ever expanding and open ended, and how the ideas of science can be appreciated just as you can appreciate a piece of music.

Tonight's music is easy, relaxed and quiet. All suggestions welcome.

Dr. Satyajit Mayor will be on tonight.

He is an acclaimed cell biologist and also the director of the National Centre for Biological Sciences in Bangalore.

The principle driving force of his laboratory is to uncover physico-chemical rules that govern local organization of the cell membrane and connect this to cellular and organismal physiology.

Special Guest

Dr Satyajit Mayor / Cell Biologist / Director of National Centre for Biological Sciences / Dr. Satyajit Mayor describes the corona virus and how it is different from any other ordinary virus.
